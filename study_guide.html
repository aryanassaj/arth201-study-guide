PATTERN: Medieval Italian Art

  • Florence (Giotto) = WEIGHT, VOLUME, EMOTION, naturalism, fresco
  • Siena (Duccio, Martini, Lorenzetti) = ELEGANCE, LINE, GOLD, decorative, tempera on wood
  • Technique: Tempera on wood panels (altarpieces) and fresco (wall painting)
  • Gold backgrounds = divine, heavenly realm (Byzantine tradition)
  • Shift: From flat Byzantine → 3D space and human emotion

PATTERN: Northern European Art

  • OIL PAINTING = rich color, luminous glazes, microscopic detail (van Eyck pioneered)

PATTERN: Quattrocento Renaissance

  • Florence = center of innovation (Medici patronage)
  • Linear perspective invented by Brunelleschi, used by Masaccio

PATTERN: High Ren → Mannerism

  • High Ren (ca. 1490–1520): HARMONY, BALANCE, IDEAL BEAUTY — Leonardo (sfumato), Raphael (harmony), Michelangelo (terribilità), Titian (colorito)

  • Figura serpentinata (spiraling figure) — Giambologna

PATTERN: Baroque Northern Europe

  • FLANDERS (Catholic) = Rubens's energy, Counter-Reformation; van Dyck court portraits
  • DUTCH REPUBLIC (Protestant, merchant) = NO church commissions → portraits, genre, landscapes, still life
  • Rembrandt = light/shadow as emotion; portraits, biblical, prints
  • Vermeer = domestic interiors; camera obscura; quiet luminous scenes
  • Hals = spontaneous brushwork; group portraits
  • Vanitas/Pronkstilleven = moral reminders amid wealth (Claesz, Kalf)
  • Landscape = pride in homeland (Cuyp, Ruisdael)
  • FRANCE = Louis XIV absolutism; Versailles; classicism (Poussin, Claude); "grand manner"
  • ENGLAND = Palladian architecture (Jones, Wren)

Study Strategy

Phase 1: Period Recognition (Days 1–2)

  1. Go through each period section above
  2. For each artwork, Google the image (artist + title) and study it for 30 seconds
  3. Focus on the VISUAL FEEL of each period — you'll start seeing patterns

Phase 2: Artist Signatures (Days 3–4)

  1. Use the Style Signatures table above
  2. Cover the left column, look at right, and guess the artist
  3. Then reverse: cover right, describe what their work looks like
  4. This is KEY for the "unknowns" section

Phase 3: Comparison Drilling (Days 5–6)

  1. Use the Comparison Pairs section
  2. For each pair, write out: artist, title, date, period, style differences, iconography
  3. Practice explaining WHY they're different (period, patron, purpose, technique)

Phase 4: Flashcard Mode (Day 7+)

  1. Use the tables as flashcards: cover columns and quiz yourself
  2. Focus on: Artist ↔ Title ↔ Date ↔ Medium ↔ Period
  3. Pay special attention to ICONOGRAPHY for symbolic meaning

Pro Tips for Your Exam

  • Part 1 (Compare/Contrast): Structure as: identify both → period/style → visual differences → iconography
  • Part 2 (Topic Question): Know 3–4 examples per major concept (perspective, patronage, Counter-Reformation, etc.)
  • Part 3 (Unknowns): Use the style signature table — look at medium, composition, color, subject, emotion level
  • Date ranges matter more than exact dates — know centuries and quarter-centuries
  • Remember the PROGRESSION: Medieval (flat/gold) → Early Ren (perspective) → High Ren (ideal beauty) → Mannerism (distortion) → Baroque (drama/emotion)
